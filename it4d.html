About Nepal

Over the years, cultures and traditions from different parts of Nepal have intermingled at its capital. The art and architecture of the Kathmandu Valley is distinctly flavored by this intellectual mix. At the center of the valley lies Basantapur Durbar Square. The Malla kings built the square between 12th and the 18th century AD and houses innumerable pagodas, courtyards, temples and the Kumari House (abode of the Living Goddess). The Pashupatinath Temple, an abode of Lord Shiva lies about 5 km northeast of the center by the airport. Swayambhunath Stupa, the holiest shrine for Buddhists in Nepal sits on Padmachala hill 3 km to the west of the city center. Boudhanath Stupa, the largest stupa rising 36 metres falls along the ancient trade route between Kathmandu and Lhasa, in Tibet 4 km northwest of the Durbar Square. Thamel, is the main tourist hub where anyone can find innumerable ways to have fun. There are several places such as Nagarkot, Dhulikhel, Kirtipur, Daman around the valley that offer visitors with an excellent panorama of the Himalayan range and serve as an excellent location to get away from the hustle and bustle of the city. Nepal is a country of great topographical diversities.
